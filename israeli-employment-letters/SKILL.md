# Israeli Employment Letters

## Instructions

### Step 1: Identify the Document Type

Classify the user's request into one of these employment document categories:

| Document Type | Hebrew Name | Key Legal Requirements |
|--------------|------------|----------------------|
| Offer Letter | מכתב הצעת עבודה | Must include salary, position, start date, benefits |
| Employment Agreement | הסכם העסקה | Full terms per Chok Hodaat La'Oved, 5762-2002 |
| Section 14 Letter | מכתב סעיף 14 | Specific language per 1998 General Approval |
| Keren Hishtalmut Notice | הודעה על קרן השתלמות | Tax benefit terms, vesting period |
| Termination Letter | מכתב פיטורין | Notice period, last day, rights summary |
| Resignation Acceptance | אישור קבלת התפטרות | Confirm date, mention pidyon chofsha |
| Trial Period Letter | מכתב תקופת ניסיון | Max 12 months, cannot extend |
| Non-Compete Agreement | הסכם אי-תחרות | Must be reasonable in scope and duration |
| Salary Update Letter | מכתב עדכון שכר | New terms, effective date |
| Employment Confirmation | אישור העסקה | For banks, visa, government agencies |

### Step 2: Apply Legal Requirements

**Employment Agreement Requirements (Chok Hodaat La'Oved, 5762-2002):**

The employer must provide a written notice within 30 days of start, including:
- Employee's full name and Teudat Zehut number
- Employer name, registration number, and address
- Start date of employment
- Job title and main duties description
- Name of direct supervisor (or their title)
- Salary amount, components breakdown, and payment date (no later than the 9th of following month per Chok Hagnat HaSachar)
- Standard working hours/week
- Weekly rest day
- Pension arrangement details
- Applicable collective agreement (hezek kibbutzi), if any

**Section 14 Letter (Siman 14 Le'Chok Pitzuei Piturin):**

The Section 14 arrangement requires specific language based on the 1998 General Approval (Ishur Klali) of the Minister of Labor. The letter must state:

1. The employer waives the right to a refund of pension/insurance contributions upon employee resignation
2. The employee agrees that employer contributions (at minimum 8.33% of salary) constitute full severance under Chok Pitzuei Piturin
3. The arrangement applies from the start date (or specified date) of employment
4. The pension contributions are to a Keren Pensia Makifah, Bituach Menahalim, or Kupat Gemel
5. Both parties acknowledge this is per the General Approval under Section 14

Key rates for Section 14 arrangements:
- Employer severance component: 8.33% of salary
- Employer pension component: 6.5% of salary (total employer: 14.83%)
- Employee pension contribution: 6% of salary
- Optional: Keren Hishtalmut employer 7.5% + employee 2.5%

**Keren Hishtalmut (Education Fund) Notice:**

Tax benefits under Pikadon Le'Keren Hishtalmut regulations:
- Employee contribution: up to 2.5% of salary (tax-deductible)
- Employer contribution: up to 7.5% of salary (tax-exempt up to ceiling)
- Monthly salary ceiling for tax benefit: 15,712 NIS (updated periodically)
- Vesting period: 6 years for tax-free withdrawal (3 years if used for education)
- For self-employed: up to 4.5% of income (deductible), additional 1.5% (credit)

**Trial Period (Tkufat Nisayon):**
- No statutory maximum, but courts recognize up to 6 months as reasonable (12 months in exceptional roles)
- Employee has full rights during trial period (pension, sick days, vacation accrue)
- Shorter notice period during trial is common (typically 7-14 days)
- Trial cannot be retroactively extended
- Labor Court (Beit Din La'Avoda) scrutinizes dismissals during trial for improper motives

**Non-Compete (Tnai Mugbal / Haskama Le'Ai Tachru):**
- Israeli Supreme Court (Beit HaMishpat HaElyon) in Tzuk Or Ltd. v. Katz set strict limits
- Must protect a legitimate interest (trade secrets, client lists, unique training)
- Reasonable in: geographic scope, time duration (6-12 months typical), industry scope
- Employee must receive meaningful consideration (tmurah mamashit) beyond regular salary
- Overbroad clauses will be narrowed or struck down by courts
- Cannot prevent employee from earning a livelihood (zchut le'chofesh ha'isuk)

### Step 3: Generate the Document

Structure each document with:

1. **Header**: Company name, logo placeholder, date, reference number
2. **Recipient**: Employee name, ID number, address
3. **Subject line**: Clear document title in Hebrew
4. **Body**: Organized by sections with specific terms
5. **Legal references**: Cite relevant laws by name and section
6. **Signatures**: Employer authorized signatory + employee acknowledgment
7. **Copies**: Note distribution (personnel file, employee, pension company)

### Step 4: Pension Arrangement Comparison

When drafting pension-related letters, explain the options:

| Feature | Keren Pensia Makifah | Bituach Menahalim | Kupat Gemel |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Disability insurance | Included | Separate rider | Not included |
| Survivors' pension | Included | Separate rider | Not included |
| Investment management fees | ~0.2-0.5% | ~0.5-1.5% | ~0.5-1.0% |
| Employer contribution | 6.5% + 8.33% | 6.5% + 8.33% | 6.5% + 8.33% |
| Employee contribution | 6% | 6% | 6% |
| Tax benefit | Section 47 | Section 45 | Limited |
| Recommended for | Most employees | High earners needing customization | Savings only |

## Examples

### Example 1: Section 14 Letter
**Input**: "Draft a Section 14 letter for a new employee starting March 1, salary 18,000 NIS"
**Output**: A formal letter in Hebrew including: employer and employee details, reference to the General Approval of 1998, employer's commitment to deposit 8.33% of 18,000 NIS (1,499.40 NIS) monthly as severance component, employee's acknowledgment that these deposits constitute full severance under Section 14, specification that deposits go to the employee's chosen Keren Pensia, effective from March 1, signatures for both parties.

### Example 2: Termination Letter
**Input**: "I need to fire an employee who worked for 2 years with a salary of 12,000 NIS"
**Output**: A termination letter including: termination date, notice period per Chok Hodaa Mukdemet (30 days for 2-year employee), last working day, severance entitlement (24,000 NIS or per Section 14 arrangement), pidyon chofsha (unused vacation days payment), instruction to return company property, hearing opportunity (shimu'a) requirement note, employee's right to unemployment benefits (dmei avtala) from Bituach Leumi after 90-day waiting period.

### Example 3: Keren Hishtalmut Setup
**Input**: "Set up Keren Hishtalmut notification for employee, salary 20,000 NIS"
**Output**: A notification letter detailing: employer contribution of 7.5% (1,500 NIS/month), employee contribution of 2.5% (500 NIS/month), tax-exempt ceiling note (benefit applies up to 15,712 NIS of salary; above that, employer contribution is taxable benefit), chosen fund name placeholder, 6-year vesting for tax-free withdrawal, 3-year vesting for educational purposes, effective date, both signatures.

## Troubleshooting

- **Issue**: User unsure whether to use Keren Pensia or Bituach Menahalim
  **Solution**: Recommend Keren Pensia Makifah for most employees (lower fees, built-in disability and survivors' coverage). Bituach Menahalim suits higher earners who want customizable coverage or already have existing policies. Always recommend consulting a pension advisor (yo'etz pensioni).

- **Issue**: Non-compete clause too broad
  **Solution**: Narrow the scope citing Tzuk Or v. Katz precedent. Limit to: specific competitors (not entire industry), 6-12 months maximum, specific geographic area, and only if the employee received special training or access to genuine trade secrets. Include a gardening leave (chofsha betuchelet) payment if enforcing strictly.

- **Issue**: Employee disputes Section 14 arrangement
  **Solution**: Section 14 requires a written, signed agreement. If the employee did not sign, the arrangement may not be valid. Check: (a) was there a signed Section 14 letter? (b) were deposits actually made at 8.33%? (c) does the collective agreement override? If disputed, recommend Beit Din La'Avoda (Labor Court).
