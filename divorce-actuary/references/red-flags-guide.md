# Red Flags Guide — Divorce Asset Concealment Patterns

Consult when: analyzing bank transactions for suspicious activity, evaluating post-separation conduct, or advising attorney on targeted discovery requests.

## Category 1 — Pre-Separation Account Setup (🔴 High Risk)

| Pattern | Signal | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| New bank account opened 1–6 months before separation | Classic asset-shielding preparation | Request full history from account opening date |
| New digital bank account (e.g., ONE ZERO, Pepper) | Harder to trace, less paper trail | Demand statements from day one |
| Extra-banking account (Tria, savings clubs) | Not automatically reported; balance hard to verify | Demand balance certificate at yom hakera |
| Provident fund or investment account opened near separation | May receive transferred assets | Cross-check with all outgoing transfers |

**Key finding from practice**: In one case, a husband opened a new digital bank account 3 months before yom hakera and transferred ₪35,000 to it — including ₪10,000 on the yom hakera itself.

---

## Category 2 — Post-Separation Asset Depletion (🔴 Critical)

These patterns indicate deliberate reduction of marital assets after separation.

| Pattern | Amount Threshold | Action |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Locking funds in fixed-term deposit after separation | Any amount | Demand deposit terms; request injunction |
| Transfers to unidentified account after yom hakera | Any amount | Court order to identify account holder |
| Credit card debt paid from joint accounts after separation | >₪5,000 | Claim reimbursement for post-separation share |
| Furniture/property purchase day after separation | Any amount | Document for asset depletion claim |
| Account "zeroed out" by end of month after separation | Any amount | Trace every transaction |

**Key finding from practice**: All transfers to an unidentified account (99400) occurred only after yom hakera — zero transfers before separation — strengthening the case for deliberate post-separation asset shielding.

---

## Category 3 — Children's Accounts as Shield (🔴 High Risk)

| Pattern | Risk | Verification |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Securities purchased in children's names within 14 days of yom hakera | Very high — textbook concealment | Demand proof of prior intention to save for children |
| Custodial account balance suddenly increases near separation | High | Trace source of funds |
| Large transfer to child's account framed as "education fund" | Medium–High | Verify when the account was opened |

**Key finding from practice**: Securities purchases of ~₪52,669 were made in children's names exactly 14 days before yom hakera. Both purchases were made simultaneously, with no prior documentation of intent.

---

## Category 4 — Recurring Transfers to Unknown Recipients

| Frequency | Typical Purpose | Risk |
|-----------|----------------|------|
| Monthly identical amount | Rent payment — verify recipient | Low if explained |
| Irregular varying amounts | Unknown — flag all | High |
| All transfers only after separation | Deliberate post-separation transfer | Very high |

---

## Category 5 — Extra-Banking and Off-Grid Assets

Israeli extra-banking entities that may hold undisclosed assets:
- **Tria** (Kehila Pina'it) — cooperative savings, balances not in bank statements
- **Gmach** loans — informal, often undocumented
- **PayPal / Bit / PayBox** — peer-to-peer, identify recipients
- **Digital wallets** — Pepper, Payoneer, foreign accounts

For each: request balance certificate specifically dated to yom hakera.

---

## Category 6 — Income Suppression

| Pattern | Method | Detection |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Sudden salary drop before separation | Voluntary demotion or deferral | Compare last 3 years of pay slips |
| Shift from salary to dividends | Self-employed or company owner | Request corporate financial statements |
| Cash income not deposited | Freelance, rental income | Look for gaps between lifestyle and deposits |
| "Loans" to friends or relatives | Disguised asset transfer | Any loan above ₪10,000 requires documentation |

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Pattern | Recommended Action |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| 🔴 Immediate | New account + transfers at yom hakera | Court injunction request |
| 🔴 Immediate | Unidentified account post-separation | Court order to identify holder |
| 🔴 Immediate | Securities in children's names 14 days before | Challenge exclusion from balance |
| 🟠 Investigate | Post-separation credit card payments from joint assets | Reimbursement claim |
| 🟠 Investigate | Extra-banking balance not in report | Demand balance certificate |
| 🟡 Monitor | High cash withdrawals | Request explanation |
| 🟡 Monitor | PayPal to unknown recipient | Identify recipient |
