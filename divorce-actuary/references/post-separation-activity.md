# Post-Separation Activity Analysis

Consult when: analyzing transactions that occurred after yom hakera (the separation date), evaluating whether post-separation conduct warrants additional claims, or advising on injunction strategy.

## Legal Basis

Under Israeli Spouses Property Relations Law (1973), the balance of marital assets is determined as of yom hakera. Any reduction of those assets after yom hakera — without the other spouse's consent — may constitute grounds for:
1. An additional equalization payment (פיצוי בגין בזבוז נכסים)
2. A court injunction to freeze remaining assets
3. A claim that the official yom hakera should be moved earlier

## Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Yom Hakera Balance
List every asset and its confirmed value as of yom hakera. This is the baseline.

### Step 2: Map All Post-Separation Transactions
For each transaction after yom hakera, record:

| Field | What to Record |
|-------|---------------|
| Date | Exact date relative to yom hakera (Day +1, Day +30, etc.) |
| Amount | In ₪ |
| Direction | Into account / out of account |
| Recipient/Sender | Name, account number if outgoing |
| Classification | Routine / Suspicious / Requires explanation |

### Step 3: Classify Each Transaction

**Routine (does not affect equalization):**
- Regular salary deposits
- Ongoing household expenses (food, utilities, insurance)
- Pension contributions continuing per contract
- Government benefits (military reserve pay, tax refunds for pre-separation period)

**Suspicious (flag for investigation):**
- Transfers to unidentified accounts
- Fixed-deposit lock-ins after separation
- Large one-time purchases
- Credit card payments from joint assets for post-separation personal expenses

### Step 4: Calculate Net Impact

```
יתרה ביום הקרע (מאומתת)           = A
סה"כ תנועות יוצאות לאחר הקרע       = B
מתוכן: הוצאות שוטפות לגיטימיות     = C
תנועות חשודות / לא מוסברות          = B − C
השפעה על תשלום האיזון               = (B − C) / 2
```

## Warning Signs Checklist

- [ ] Any transfer to an account not appearing in pre-separation documents
- [ ] Fixed deposit opened after separation (locks funds from seizure)
- [ ] Credit card payment from joint account for post-separation personal spending
- [ ] Total outflows after separation exceed 20% of yom hakera balance
- [ ] Account balance at end of month is significantly lower than yom hakera balance without legitimate explanation
- [ ] All transfers to a specific account occur only post-separation (zero pre-separation transfers)

## Recommended Attorney Actions

When post-separation depletion is identified:
1. File for **tzav ikul** (עיקול) on remaining accounts
2. Request court order to identify holders of unknown recipient accounts
3. Demand explanation for each suspicious transaction in writing
4. Consider motion to adjust equalization amount upward by the depleted sum
5. If pattern is severe, petition to revisit yom hakera date
