# Israeli car-accident claim: full guide

The one rule to keep straight: bodily injury is no-fault, property damage is fault-based. Everything below follows from that split.

## The three insurance types

| Policy | Hebrew | Mandatory | Covers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Compulsory | ביטוח חובה | Yes | Bodily injury only (driver, passengers, people outside the vehicle). Never property. |
| Comprehensive | ביטוח מקיף | No | Your own vehicle's damage (collision, theft, fire, flood, vandalism) plus liability for third-party property. |
| Third-party property | ביטוח צד ג' | No | Damage you cause to someone else's property. Not your own vehicle. |

## Bodily injury: no-fault (PLATD)

Under the Road Accident Victims Compensation Law, 1975 (חוק הפלת"ד), the vehicle user must compensate a person injured in a road accident involving the vehicle, regardless of fault. In practice each injured person (driver, passengers, pedestrians) claims from the compulsory insurance (ביטוח חובה) of the vehicle they were in or were struck by. Fault is irrelevant to the injury claim.

A road accident for this law is an event in which a person suffers bodily injury due to use of a motor vehicle for transport purposes.

A serious or lasting-injury claim is assessed by a medical committee and decided by the courts, and usually needs a lawyer. The law does not require a lawyer to start a claim, but for anything beyond minor injury you should consult one. This guide helps document and understand rights; it does not replace a lawyer.

## Property damage: fault-based

Property liability follows fault: whoever caused the damage, or their insurer, pays.

- If you are at fault: the other party claims against your third-party (צד ג') cover. Your own car's damage is covered only if you hold comprehensive (מקיף).
- If you are not at fault: claim from your own comprehensive and pay the deductible (השתתפות עצמית); your insurer recovers from the at-fault party by subrogation (שיבוב) under the Insurance Contract Law, and refunds your deductible once it is paid in full. Or claim directly against the at-fault driver's third-party cover, or sue them (small claims for smaller sums).

## At the scene

- Safety first: hazard lights, warning triangle, move to safety only if drivable and nobody is hurt.
- Check for injuries; call emergency services if anyone is hurt.
- Photograph all vehicles, the damage, plates, the road position, signage, and skid marks.
- Exchange details: name, ID, phone, plate, and the other driver's insurer and policy number.
- Collect witnesses' names and phones.

The duty to exchange details on the spot applies to every accident (תקנה 145). An injury accident adds the duties to stop, render aid, and report to the police (תקנה 144). Failing to stop or to summon aid where a person was injured is a serious criminal offense under the Traffic Ordinance (Section 64א): up to 7 years imprisonment, rising to up to 14 years where the injury is serious or fatal.

## Who claims from which insurer (the allocation rule)

For bodily injury the carrier rule (PLATD Section 3) decides which compulsory insurer pays:

- A driver and the passengers in a vehicle claim against that vehicle's own compulsory insurance.
- In a multi-vehicle crash, each driver's own bodily injury goes to his own vehicle's insurer, regardless of who was at fault.
- A pedestrian or cyclist injured outside the vehicles claims against the vehicle that hit them (and where several vehicles were involved, against the involved drivers jointly).

So if two cars collide, each driver's injuries are covered by their own car's חובה. Do not chase the other driver's compulsory insurer for your own injuries.

## Who is NOT entitled (PLATD Section 7)

The no-fault rule has hard exceptions. A person is NOT entitled to bodily-injury compensation under PLATD if they: caused the accident deliberately; drove a vehicle without the owner's permission or a stolen vehicle (or rode in it knowing this); drove with no valid driving licence (a licence that merely lapsed for non-payment of the fee still counts as valid); used the vehicle to commit a felony; or drove with no valid compulsory insurance. If any of these applies, the confident "you are covered regardless of fault" does not hold, and the user needs a lawyer to assess their position.

## Preserve the medical paper trail

For an injury, the medical documentation is what wins or loses the claim, more than the car photos. Anyone hurt should be examined the same day (ER or doctor), describe every affected body part to the doctor, and keep every medical record. An injury mentioned for the first time weeks later is easy for the insurer or Karnit to dispute.

## If it happened on the way to or from work

An accident on the way to or from work, or during work, may also be a work accident (תאונת עבודה). Report it to the National Insurance Institute (ביטוח לאומי) as well, because it can trigger separate rights and affects the calculation of the PLATD claim.

## The police confirmation

A police confirmation (אישור משטרתי) is a precondition for filing the insurance claim. When there are no injuries (or injured people were treated and released within 24 hours), file the gov.il online light-accident report and receive the confirmation by email. In more serious cases report by phone or in person.

## Hit-and-run, untraced, or uninsured: Karnit

Karnit (קרנית), the Fund for Compensating Road Accident Victims established under PLATD, pays bodily-injury compensation when you cannot claim from an insurer: the driver is untraced (פגע וברח), the at-fault vehicle had no insurance, or the at-fault insurer is in liquidation. Karnit is for the injury side, not for repairing your car.

## The accident report (הודעה על תאונה)

The standard insurer report is one-sided, filed with your own insurer (Israel has no joint two-driver constat-amiable form). It captures: the insured and policy details, the driver, the accident details with a scene diagram, the impact zones, and witnesses, the third-party details, and the insured's signed declaration. Notify the insurer immediately after the accident, then submit the form with the photos, the police confirmation, and a damage assessment.

## Time limits

- Report to the insurer immediately after the accident (Insurance Contract Law Section 22; standard policy Section 27(a)).
- Statute of limitations: 7 years for both property and PLATD bodily-injury claims (Limitation Law Section 5). A minor injured in an accident can sue until about age 25 (Section 10).

## Disputes and escalation

If the insurer underpays or rejects unfairly, complain to the Public Inquiries Unit at the Capital Market, Insurance and Savings Authority. Filing the complaint does not bar going to court; for smaller property sums, file in small claims (route to israeli-small-claims-court).

## Advanced

- Subrogation (שיבוב / תחלוף): the mechanism by which a not-at-fault driver's insurer recovers from the at-fault party after paying the claim.
- Total loss: when the repair cost is high relative to the vehicle's value, the insurer declares a total loss (אובדן גמור) and pays the vehicle's worth instead of repairing. A car can also be declared a constructive total loss (אובדן להלכה), which gets a permanent note in the vehicle registry that lowers its resale value, so check how the insurer classified the car and what it does to future value.
- Diminished value (ירידת ערך): the drop in the car's value after a repaired accident is a recognized head of damage against the at-fault party, but it needs an assessor (שמאי) appraisal to prove. Rental and loss-of-use are also recoverable from the at-fault party, with proof.
