# Israeli Rental Contract Review Checklist

A detailed checklist of every clause to verify in an Israeli residential rental contract (chozeh schirut, חוזה שכירות), with explanations of common pitfalls and key Hebrew terms.

## Before Signing: Pre-Contract Verification

### Landlord Identity and Ownership

| Check | Hebrew Term | What to Do | Common Pitfall |
|-------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| Verify landlord identity | זהות המשכיר | Request copy of Teudat Zehut | Signing with someone who is not the actual owner |
| Confirm property ownership | בעלות על הנכס | Request Tabu extract (nesach tabu, נסח טאבו) | Property may have liens or multiple owners |
| Check for power of attorney | ייפוי כוח | If signing with agent, verify notarized POA | Unauthorized representatives signing on behalf of owner |
| Verify no bankruptcy proceedings | הליכי פשיטת רגל | Check with Execution Office (Hotza'a LaPo'al) | Landlord under bankruptcy cannot legally lease |

### Property Verification

| Check | Hebrew Term | What to Do | Common Pitfall |
|-------|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| Confirm address matches records | כתובת בטאבו | Compare contract address with Tabu extract | Address discrepancies between documents |
| Verify zoning is residential | ייעוד מגורים | Check with municipality (iriya) | Commercial property rented as residential |
| Check Arnona classification | סיווג ארנונה | Visit iriya website or office | Wrong classification leads to higher bills |
| Inspect for structural issues | בעיות מבניות | Walk through and photograph everything | Hidden water damage, mold, or structural cracks |

## Section 1: Party Details (Pratey HaTzedadim, פרטי הצדדים)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Landlord full name | שם מלא של המשכיר | Matches Teudat Zehut exactly | Name differs from Tabu registration |
| Landlord ID number | מספר ת.ז. המשכיר | 9-digit Israeli ID | Missing or partial ID |
| Landlord contact info | פרטי קשר המשכיר | Phone, email, address | No alternative contact method |
| Tenant full name | שם מלא של השוכר | Matches your Teudat Zehut | Misspellings can cause legal issues |
| Tenant ID number | מספר ת.ז. השוכר | Your 9-digit ID | Ensure accuracy |
| Additional tenants | דיירים נוספים | All residents should be listed | Unnamed residents may have no legal standing |

## Section 2: Property Description (Teur HaNechess, תיאור הנכס)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Exact address | כתובת מדויקת | Street, building number, apartment number, floor | Vague address description |
| Number of rooms | מספר חדרים | Count and verify (Israeli "rooms" exclude kitchen/bathroom) | "3 rooms" may be interpreted differently |
| Included fixtures | מתקנים כלולים | AC units, water heater (dud shemesh/dud chashmal), oven, etc. | Fixtures removed before move-in |
| Parking space | חניה | Space number, location, covered/uncovered | Verbal promise of parking not in contract |
| Storage room (machsan) | מחסן | Location, size, accessibility | Storage not actually allocated to this unit |
| Furniture (if furnished) | ריהוט | Itemized list with condition | No inventory means disputes at move-out |
| Building floor | קומה | Exact floor number | Important for insurance and accessibility |

## Section 3: Lease Term (Tkufat HaSchirut, תקופת השכירות)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Start date | תאריך התחלה | Exact date you receive keys | Gap between payment start and key handover |
| End date | תאריך סיום | Exact date, typically 12 months later | Ambiguous end date |
| Option clause (optzia) | אופציה | Terms for renewal, rent for option period | No option, or option with undefined rent |
| Option exercise deadline | מועד אחרון להפעלת אופציה | When you must notify landlord of renewal | Very early notification deadline (e.g., 90+ days) |
| Handover date | מועד מסירה | Date and time for key exchange | No specific handover arrangement |

### Common Pitfalls in Lease Terms

- **No option clause**: Without an option, the landlord can refuse to renew or demand a large rent increase at lease end.
- **Automatic renewal without notice**: Some contracts auto-renew unless you give notice 60-90 days before end. Missing this deadline locks you in.
- **Option with "market rate" rent**: If the option clause says rent adjusts to "market rate" (mechir shuk), there is no ceiling on the increase. Insist on a specific amount or formula.

## Section 4: Rent (Dmey Schirut, דמי שכירות)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Monthly amount | סכום חודשי | Exact NIS amount, in words and numbers | Amount unclear or inconsistent |
| Payment date | מועד תשלום | Day of month (e.g., 1st, 10th) | No specified date |
| Payment method | אופן תשלום | Bank transfer, checks, cash | Cash only (no paper trail) |
| Late payment penalty | קנס פיגור | Reasonable penalty clause | Excessive daily penalties |
| Index linking (hatzmada) | הצמדה למדד | Whether rent adjusts to CPI, base index date | Unclear base index or adjustment formula |
| Payment receipts | קבלות | Landlord must provide receipts | Contract says no receipts required |

## Section 5: Deposits and Guarantees (Arancia/Pikadon, ערנצ'ה/פיקדון)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Deposit type | סוג ערבות | Bank guarantee, checks, cash, shtar hon | Shtar hon (promissory note) without legal advice |
| Deposit amount | סכום פיקדון | Should not exceed 3 months' rent (Fair Rental Law) | Exceeds 3 months |
| Return conditions | תנאי החזרה | Specific conditions for full/partial return | Vague or one-sided return conditions |
| Return timeline | לוח זמנים להחזרה | Should be within 60 days of lease end | No specified timeline or over 60 days |
| Deduction rules | כללי ניכוי | Itemized deductions with documentation required | Landlord has unlimited deduction rights |
| Receipt for deposit | קבלה על פיקדון | Written receipt upon deposit payment | No receipt provided |

### Deposit Protection Best Practices

1. **Always get a signed receipt** (kabala, קבלה) listing the exact amount and date
2. **Photograph the apartment** on move-in day with timestamps. Send photos to the landlord by email for a documented record
3. **Create a property condition report** (protokol matzav dira, פרוטוקול מצב דירה) and attach it to the contract as an appendix (nispach, נספח)
4. **For bank guarantees**: ensure the guarantee document specifies the same conditions as the contract for releasing funds
5. **Never agree to a shtar hon** without consulting a lawyer. A shtar hon can be enforced as debt through the Execution Office without a court hearing

## Section 6: Maintenance and Repairs (Tachzuka v'Tikunim, תחזוקה ותיקונים)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Landlord structural repairs | תיקוני מבנה של המשכיר | Roof, walls, plumbing, electrical, water heater | Contract shifts structural costs to tenant |
| Tenant maintenance | תחזוקה שוטפת של השוכר | Day-to-day upkeep, light bulbs, minor fixes | Tenant responsible for everything |
| Repair cost threshold | סף עלות תיקון | Amount above which landlord pays | No threshold defined |
| Response time for repairs | זמן תגובה לתיקונים | How quickly landlord must respond | No response time obligation |
| Emergency repairs | תיקוני חירום | Tenant can arrange emergency repairs | No provision for emergencies |
| Appliance responsibility | אחריות מכשירים | Who repairs/replaces included appliances | Unclear ownership of appliances |

### Fair Rental Law Requirements

Under the Fair Rental Law 2017 (Chok Schirut Hogenet, חוק שכירות הוגנת):
- The **landlord** must maintain structural elements: roof, exterior walls, plumbing systems, electrical wiring, water heating systems
- The **tenant** is responsible for day-to-day maintenance and minor repairs resulting from normal use
- Any clause that shifts ALL repair costs to the tenant may be challenged as violating the law

## Section 7: Utilities and Municipal Charges (Cheshbonot v'Tashlumim Ironiyim)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Arnona (municipal tax) | ארנונה | Who pays (almost always tenant) | Arnona remains in landlord's name but tenant pays |
| Va'ad bayit (building committee) | ועד בית | Amount, what it covers, who pays | Tenant pays but has no say in building decisions |
| Electricity (chashmal) | חשמל | Transfer to tenant's name | Meter stays in landlord's name |
| Water (mayim) | מים | Transfer to tenant's name or shared meter | No individual meter |
| Gas (gaz) | גז | Type (natural gas or balloon), responsibility | Gas balloon replacement responsibility unclear |
| Internet/Cable | אינטרנט/כבלים | Who arranges and pays | Pre-existing contract tenant must maintain |

### Important Notes on Utilities

- **Transfer accounts to your name** wherever possible. If utilities remain in the landlord's name, you may face disputes over unpaid bills from previous periods.
- **Check Arnona classification** before signing. Residential rates differ by area, property size, and municipality. An incorrect classification can mean overpaying by hundreds of shekels monthly.
- **Va'ad bayit fees** vary widely. Ask neighbors about the actual monthly amount and what it covers (cleaning, elevator maintenance, garden, insurance).

## Section 8: Termination and Early Exit (Siuf Biitul, סעיף ביטול)

| Clause | Hebrew | What to Verify | Red Flag |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------|
| Notice period | תקופת הודעה מוקדמת | 60-90 days is standard | Very long notice period (120+ days) |
| Early termination by tenant | ביטול מוקדם ע"י השוכר | Conditions, penalty, replacement tenant | No right to terminate early under any condition |
| Early termination by landlord | ביטול מוקדם ע"י המשכיר | Must have legal grounds | Landlord can terminate at will |
| Penalty for early exit | קנס ביטול מוקדם | Should be reasonable | Penalty equals remaining rent |
| Finding a replacement | מציאת מחליף | Process and approval conditions | Landlord has unreasonable approval power |
| Return condition | מצב החזרה | "Normal wear and tear" (blai savir) clause | "Perfect condition" requirement |

## Section 9: Special Clauses to Watch For

| Clause | Hebrew | Concern |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Non-compete / exclusive use | שימוש בלעדי | Restrictions on how you use the property |
| Subletting prohibition | איסור השכרת משנה | May be too restrictive |
| Pet clause | סעיף חיות מחמד | If you have pets, ensure it is permitted |
| Renovation restrictions | הגבלות שיפוצים | Cannot hang pictures, paint, etc. |
| Landlord entry rights | זכות כניסה למשכיר | Must require notice (24 hours minimum) |
| Assignment of rights | המחאת זכויות | Landlord selling property during lease |
| Governing law and jurisdiction | דין חל וסמכות | Should be Israeli law, local court |

## Final Pre-Signing Checklist

Before putting pen to paper:

- [ ] Read the entire contract (every clause, every appendix)
- [ ] Verify landlord identity and property ownership
- [ ] Photograph the apartment with timestamps
- [ ] Create and sign a property condition report
- [ ] Confirm all financial terms (rent, deposit, utilities)
- [ ] Understand termination and renewal conditions
- [ ] Check for any blank spaces that could be filled in later
- [ ] Get a signed copy for yourself
- [ ] Consider having a lawyer review the contract (cost: 500-1,500 NIS)
