# Israeli Labor Law

## Instructions

### Step 1: Identify the Legal Topic

When a user asks about Israeli employment or labor law, classify their question into one of these major areas:

| Area | Primary Law | Key Reference |
|------|------------|---------------|
| Minimum Wage | Chok Sachar Minimum, 5747-1987 | Current rate: 5,880.02 NIS/month (as of April 2024) |
| Working Hours | Chok Shaat Avoda U'Menucha, 5711-1951 | Max 42 hours/week, 8.6 hours/day (5-day week) |
| Severance Pay | Chok Pitzuei Piturin, 5723-1963 | One month salary per year of service |
| Annual Vacation | Chok Chayal Ha'Avoda (Chofsha Shnatit), 5711-1951 | 12-28 days based on seniority |
| Sick Days | Chok Dmei Machala, 5736-1976 | 1.5 days accrued per month, up to 90 days |
| Pregnancy | Chok Nashim, 5714-1954 (Employment of Women Law) | Firing ban from pregnancy notification |
| Pension | Tzav Harchavat Pensia Chovah, 2008 | Mandatory pension from day one of employment |
| Notice Period | Chok Hodaa Mukdemet, 5761-2001 | 1 day per month in first year, up to 30 days |

### Step 2: Provide Accurate Legal Information

Always cite the specific law name (in Hebrew transliteration and English), relevant section numbers, and current numerical values. Structure the answer as follows:

**Minimum Wage (Chok Sachar Minimum, 5747-1987)**
- Monthly: 5,880.02 NIS (effective April 2024)
- Hourly: 32.30 NIS (based on 182 hours/month)
- Applies to all employees aged 18+, including part-time (pro rata)
- Youth minimum wage: graduated scale (70% at age 16, 83% at age 17)
- Section 6: employer who pays below minimum wage commits a criminal offense

**Severance Pay (Chok Pitzuei Piturin, 5723-1963)**
- Section 1: employee dismissed after 1+ year entitled to severance
- Calculation: last month's salary x years of service
- Section 12: resignation treated as dismissal in certain cases (relocation for marriage, health, worsened conditions)
- Section 14 arrangement: employer deposits to pension/insurance fund monthly; upon termination, those deposits constitute the severance. Releases employer from further obligation if properly executed.
- Section 14 requires: (a) employer contributes at least 8.33% of salary, (b) written agreement, (c) general approval of the Minister of Labor

**Sick Days (Chok Dmei Machala, 5736-1976)**
- Accrual: 1.5 days per month of employment (18 days/year)
- Maximum accumulation: 90 days
- Payment structure: Day 1 = unpaid; Days 2-3 = 50% of daily wage; Day 4+ = 100% of daily wage
- Requires medical certificate (ishur machala) from day 1
- Section 4a: employer may not deduct sick days from vacation days

**Annual Vacation (Chok Chayal Ha'Avoda)**
- Years 1-4: 12 working days (10 net for 5-day week)
- Year 5: 14 days (12 net)
- Year 6: 16 days (14 net)
- Year 7: 18 days (15 net)
- Year 8+: 1 additional day per year, up to maximum 28 days
- Calculation for partial year: (days worked / 200) x annual entitlement (or /240 for 6-day week)
- Vacation buyout (pidyon chofsha) on termination: only unused days from current + prior year

**Working Hours (Chok Shaat Avoda U'Menucha, 5711-1951)**
- Standard: 8 hours/day, 42 hours/week (since April 2018, previously 43)
- Overtime: first 2 hours at 125%, subsequent hours at 150%
- Weekly rest (menucha shvuit): minimum 36 consecutive hours, including Shabbat for Jewish employees
- Night shift (10 PM - 6 AM): maximum 7 hours, 125% pay minimum
- Section 30b: Heter Pikuach (supervision exemption) for certain managers/trust positions

**Pregnancy and Maternity (Chok Nashim, 5714-1954)**
- Section 9: absolute prohibition on firing pregnant employee without Heter (permit) from MAMAT (Ministry of Labor)
- Protection begins from day one of employment
- Maternity leave: 26 weeks (15 paid by Bituach Leumi if 10+ months of insurance payments)
- Maternity pay (dmei leda): based on average of last 3 months' salary, capped
- Section 7(c): fertility treatment protections (absences for treatments)
- Father's paternity leave: 5 days (3 from vacation days, 2 unpaid) after birth

**Mandatory Pension (Tzav Harchavat Pensia, 2008/2017)**
- Applies to all employees from day one of employment (retroactively after 6 months, or immediately if had prior pension)
- Employer contribution: 6.5% of salary (pension) + 6% (severance component)
- Employee contribution: 6% of salary
- Funds go to Keren Pensia, Bituach Menahalim, or Keren Hishtalmut (education fund is separate, not mandatory)

### Step 4: Provide Calculation Formulas

When relevant, provide formulas:

**Severance calculation:**
```
Severance = Last Monthly Salary x Years of Service
Partial year: (months / 12) x Last Monthly Salary
```

**Vacation buyout on termination:**
```
Daily rate = Monthly Salary / 21.67 (5-day week) or / 25 (6-day week)
Buyout = Unused Days x Daily Rate
```

**Sick day payment:**
```
Day 1: 0 NIS
Days 2-3: (Monthly Salary / 25) x 0.5
Day 4+: Monthly Salary / 25
```

**Overtime:**
```
Regular hourly rate = Monthly Salary / 186 (5-day week) or / 182
First 2 OT hours: Rate x 1.25
Additional OT hours: Rate x 1.5
```

## Examples

### Example 1: Severance with Section 14
**Input**: "I was fired after 3 years. My salary is 15,000 NIS. My employer has Section 14. What do I get?"
**Output**: Under Section 14 of Chok Pitzuei Piturin (5723-1963), your employer has been depositing 8.33% of your salary monthly to your pension/insurance fund as a severance component. Your statutory severance would be 15,000 x 3 = 45,000 NIS. With Section 14, the accumulated fund balance constitutes your severance. If the fund balance is lower due to market losses, you receive the fund balance (not the statutory amount). If higher, you receive the full fund balance. The employer is released from additional severance obligation per the general approval (ishur klali) of the Minister of Labor from 1998.

### Example 2: Sick Day Calculation
**Input**: "I've worked for 2 years and never took sick days. How many do I have?"
**Output**: Under Chok Dmei Machala (5736-1976), you accrue 1.5 sick days per month. After 2 years (24 months): 24 x 1.5 = 36 sick days accumulated. Maximum accumulation is 90 days. Remember: Day 1 is unpaid, Days 2-3 are at 50%, and Day 4 onward is at 100%.

### Example 3: Pregnancy Protection
**Input**: "I'm 3 months pregnant and my employer wants to fire me. Can they?"
**Output**: Under Section 9 of Chok Nashim (5714-1954, the Employment of Women Law), your employer is absolutely prohibited from firing you during pregnancy without obtaining a Heter (special permit) from the regional MAMAT (Ministry of Labor and Welfare) commissioner. This protection applies regardless of how long you have worked there. If fired without a Heter, the termination is void and you are entitled to continued employment and back pay. Your employer may face criminal charges under Section 14 of the same law.

## Troubleshooting

- **Issue**: User asks about a very specific or niche labor scenario
  **Solution**: Provide the relevant law framework, note that individual cases may vary, and recommend consulting a labor attorney (orech din le'dinei avoda) or contacting the Ministry of Labor hotline at *6545.

- **Issue**: User asks about collective agreements (heskem kibbutzi)
  **Solution**: Explain that collective agreements may provide benefits above the statutory minimum. The specific terms depend on the sector and union. Check with the relevant labor union or the Extension Orders (tzavei harchava) database.

- **Issue**: User confuses Section 14 with full pension rights
  **Solution**: Clarify that Section 14 only addresses the severance component. Pension accumulation, disability insurance, and survivors' benefits are separate pension fund features governed by the pension fund regulations and Tzav Harchavat Pensia.
