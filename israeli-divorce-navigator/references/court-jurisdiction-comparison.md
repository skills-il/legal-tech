# Jurisdiction: rabbinical court vs. family court

Israel has no civil divorce for Jews. Two forums matter, and the choice between them (plus the order of filing) is the first strategic decision.

## Who handles what

| Matter | Rabbinical court (בית דין רבני) | Family court (בית המשפט לענייני משפחה) |
|--------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| The get (גט) itself, dissolving the marriage | Exclusive. Only the rabbinical court grants a get, under Jewish law (חוק שיפוט בתי דין רבניים (נישואין וגירושין), תשי"ג-1953). | No jurisdiction over the get. |
| Property balancing (איזון משאבים) | Concurrent | Concurrent |
| Spousal maintenance (מזונות אישה) | Concurrent | Concurrent |
| Child support (מזונות ילדים) | Only with both parties' consent | Concurrent |
| Custody / parental responsibility (משמורת / אחריות הורית) | Concurrent | Concurrent |
| Get-refusal sanctions (צווי הגבלה) | Yes, under חוק בתי דין רבניים (קיום פסקי דין של גירושין), תשנ"ה-1995 | No |

## How matters get attached: joinder (כריכה), not just filing order

It is a mistake to reduce this to "whoever files first wins." The governing doctrine is joinder (כריכה): a spouse can join the ancillary matters (property, spousal maintenance, custody) to a divorce claim in the rabbinical court, and that court then hears them. A valid joinder turns on three cumulative conditions, not filing order alone:

- the divorce claim is genuine (תביעת גירושין כנה),
- the joinder is genuine (כריכה כנה),
- the joinder was done lawfully (כריכה כדין).

If all three hold, the rabbinical court acquires jurisdiction over the joined matters. Two hard limits:

- The rabbinical court has NO jurisdiction over child support (מזונות ילדים) unless BOTH parties consent, so a parent who wants child support decided in the family court is usually safe there.
- Chronology still matters, but only as a tie-breaker between the forums for a matter that was not validly joined.

Practical framing for the user (not legal advice):
- The get always goes to the rabbinical court, no matter what.
- People who want a secular, best-interests-of-the-child framing for money and children often prefer to anchor those concurrent matters in the family court, and file there first.
- People who want everything decided together in one forum sometimes prefer the rabbinical court and a joinder.
- A family lawyer should decide the actual filing and joinder strategy, because the window can close in days.

## The mandatory intake comes first either way, but urgent relief does not wait

Before litigating in either forum you generally file a Request to Resolve a Dispute (בקשה ליישוב סכסוך) under חוק להסדר התדיינויות בסכסוכי משפחה, תשע"ה-2014. The parties attend up to four MAHUT meetings (מהו"ת) at an assistance unit within 45 days, and a 60-day stay of proceedings applies.

Safety carve-out: the stay does NOT block urgent relief (סעד דחוף). Domestic violence (a צו הגנה under חוק למניעת אלימות במשפחה, התשנ"א-1991), exit-from-country, urgent maintenance, securing a minor's contact, and medical matters are express exceptions decided urgently, within about 14 days. Never route someone in danger through the intake before they can get protection. Note: filing the Request also interacts with the forum and joinder question, so where and when you file it is itself a lawyer question.
