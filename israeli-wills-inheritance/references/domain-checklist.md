# Domain Coverage Checklist (israeli-wills-inheritance)

Generated: 2026-06-18 via research on: kolzchut.org.il (צוואה בעדים, צוואה, הפקדת צוואה, צו ירושה, צו קיום צוואה), gov.il / inheritance.justice.gov.il (the Inheritance Registrar). Statute: Succession Law 1965 (חוק הירושה תשכ"ה-1965).

Scope: help an Israeli (1) draft a legally-valid will (focus on the witnessed will), (2) deposit it with the Inheritance Registrar, and (3) obtain a succession order or a probate order. Drafting binding enduring power of attorney, guardianship, and contested-estate litigation are out of scope.

## Must cover (core)
- [ ] The four will types under the Succession Law: בכתב יד (handwritten), בעדים (witnesses), בפני רשות (before an authority), בעל פה (oral / שכיב מרע). source: kolzchut צוואה. why core: the user must know which form fits.
- [ ] Witnessed will (צוואה בעדים) requirements, the skill's primary deliverable: not legally required to use a lawyer (but a lawyer is recommended); the testator writes/types the wishes, dates it, declares before two witnesses that it is their will, and signs by hand in front of them; the two witnesses confirm in writing on the will. source: kolzchut צוואה בעדים. why core: this is what the skill drafts.
- [ ] Witness disqualification: a witness, and the witness's spouse, cannot be a beneficiary of the will (a bequest to such a person is void). source: kolzchut צוואה בעדים. why core: the single most common way a self-made will is partially invalidated.
- [ ] Will deposit with the Inheritance Registrar (הרשם לענייני ירושה): optional, not required for validity, safeguards the document. source: kolzchut הפקדת צוואה. why core: users ask how to protect the will.
- [ ] Succession order (צו ירושה): filed when the deceased left NO will; heirs receive their share by law (spouse, children, parents per the Succession Law order). source: kolzchut צו ירושה. why core: the no-will path.
- [ ] Probate / will-execution order (צו קיום צוואה): filed when the deceased DID leave a will, to give it binding effect and distribute per its provisions. source: kolzchut צו קיום צוואה. why core: the with-will path.
- [ ] Where and how to apply: online to the Inheritance Registrar (inheritance.justice.gov.il), with the current fees (2026: 507 NIS application + 66 NIS publication online). source: kolzchut צו ירושה. why core: the actionable next step.

## Should cover (advanced / edge cases)
- [ ] Revoking or changing a will: a later valid will overrides an earlier one; how to update. source: Succession Law. why: common follow-up.
- [ ] Handwritten will (צוואה בכתב יד) requirements: written entirely in the testator's own hand, dated, and signed; no witnesses needed. source: kolzchut. why: the simplest no-witness option.
- [ ] Mutual / reciprocal wills for spouses (צוואה הדדית) and their special revocation rules. source: Succession Law. why: very common for couples.
- [ ] Order of legal heirs when there is no will (spouse share + children / parents / siblings per the parentela system). source: Succession Law. why: needed to explain a צו ירושה outcome.
- [ ] Objection / contest (התנגדות לצו) moves the file from the Registrar to the Family Court. source: kolzchut. why: sets expectations.

## Out of scope (explicit, with rationale)
- Enduring power of attorney (ייפוי כוח מתמשך): requires a certified, specially-trained attorney to draft and file with the Registrar. related skill: israeli-elder-care-navigator covers it.
- Guardianship (אפוטרופסות): a different court process, not part of wills/inheritance drafting.
- Contested-estate litigation between heirs: requires a licensed inheritance lawyer (עורך דין). recommend one.
- Estate / inheritance tax in OTHER countries (US estate tax, etc.): foreign-law, out of scope.
- Complex estates (business interests, foreign assets, trusts, minors' guardianship clauses): recommend a lawyer; the witnessed-will draft is for straightforward bequests.
