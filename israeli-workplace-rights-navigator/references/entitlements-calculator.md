Quick-reference tables for calculating employee entitlements based on years of employment.

### 5-Day Work Week (Multiply by 5/6, round up)

| Years of Employment | Calendar Days | Working Days |
|---------------------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | 12 | 10 |
| 2 | 12 | 10 |
| 3 | 12 | 10 |
| 4 | 12 | 10 |
| 5 | 14 | 12 |
| 6 | 16 | 14 |

**Notes:**
- Part-time employees: proportional to work percentage (e.g., 50% position gets 50% of days)
- Accumulation: unused days can roll over for up to 2 years
- Payout on termination: all unused accumulated days must be paid (pidyon chufsha)

### Daily Rate (Private Sector)

| Year | Daily Rate (NIS) |
|------|-----------------|
| 2023 | 378 |
| 2024 | 400 |
| 2025 | 418 |

**Note:** The daily rate is updated annually. Public sector employees receive a higher rate per collective agreement. Check the Ministry of Economy website for the current rate.

### Calculation Example

Employee with 5 years of seniority (2025 rate):
```
Convalescence pay = 7 days x NIS 418 = NIS 2,926
```

Part-time employee (50% position) with 5 years:
```
Convalescence pay = 7 days x NIS 418 x 50% = NIS 1,463
```

## Severance Pay (Severance Pay Law, 1963)

### Basic Formula

```
Severance = Last Monthly Salary x Years of Employment
```

### Partial Year Calculation

```
Severance = Last Monthly Salary x (Full Years + Remaining Months / 12)
```

### Quick Reference

| Years | Months | Multiplier |
|-------|--------|------------|
| 1 | 0 | 1.00 |
| 1 | 6 | 1.50 |
| 2 | 0 | 2.00 |
| 3 | 0 | 3.00 |
| 3 | 4 | 3.33 |
| 5 | 0 | 5.00 |
| 10 | 0 | 10.00 |

### Section 14 Arrangement

When Section 14 applies (most common arrangement):
- Employer contributes 8.33% of monthly salary to a pension/provident fund as severance
- Upon termination, the employee receives the **accumulated fund balance** (not the formula calculation)
- The fund balance may be higher or lower than the formula calculation depending on investment returns
- Employee retains the right to any difference if the fund balance is lower than the statutory amount (only if the employer did not fully comply with Section 14)

## Pension Contributions (Mandatory Since 2008)

| Component | Employee | Employer | Total |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------|
| Pension savings | 6.0% | 6.5% | 12.5% |
| Severance (Section 14) | -- | 8.33% | 8.33% |
| **Total** | **6.0%** | **14.83%** | **20.83%** |

**Eligibility timeline:**
- Existing pension holders: contributions start from day 1
- New employees without pension: after 6 months of employment
- Contributions calculated on gross salary up to the insurable ceiling
